# Term Insurance vs ULIP vs Endowment - Detailed Comparison

## Quick Summary

| Feature | Term Insurance | ULIP | Endowment |
|---------|---------------|------|-----------|
| Purpose | Pure Protection | Protection + Investment | Protection + Savings |
| Premium | Lowest | Medium-High | Medium |
| Death Benefit | High (10-20x premium) | Sum Assured or Fund Value | Sum Assured + Bonus |
| Maturity Benefit | NONE | Fund Value | Sum Assured + Bonus |
| Returns | No returns | Market-linked (8-14%) | Guaranteed (5-6%) |
| Lock-in | None (but pay till term) | 5 years | Till maturity |
| Flexibility | Low | High (fund switches) | Low |
| Transparency | High | Medium | Low |
| Tax on returns | N/A (no returns) | 10(10D) exempt | 10(10D) exempt |
| Recommended for | Anyone needing life cover | Long-term investors | Conservative investors |

## Detailed Comparison

### TERM INSURANCE

**What it is:**
Pure life insurance. You pay premium, if you die, family gets money. If you survive, you get nothing. That's it.

**Why it's the foundation of financial planning:**
- For Rs. 15,000/year, a 30-year-old can get Rs. 1 Crore cover for 30 years
- This Rs. 1 Crore cannot be replicated by any other product at this price
- Experts universally recommend: "Buy Term + Invest the rest"

**When to buy:**
- As soon as you have dependents (spouse, children, parents)
- Before age 35 for lowest premiums
- Coverage should be 10-15x your annual income

**Sample: LIC Tech-Term for 30-year-old male**
- Sum Assured: Rs. 1 Crore
- Policy Term: 30 years (coverage till 60)
- Annual Premium: ~Rs. 13,000/year (non-smoker)
- Monthly: ~Rs. 1,100/month

### ULIP (Unit Linked Insurance Plan)

**What it is:**
Part of your premium buys life insurance. Remaining is invested in mutual-fund-like funds. You choose equity/debt/balanced based on risk appetite.

**How it works:**
- Pay Rs. 1,00,000 annual premium
- Rs. 12,000 goes to mortality (life cover cost)
- Rs. 8,000 goes to policy admin charges
- Rs. 80,000 gets invested in your chosen fund
- After 10-15 years, fund value may be Rs. 15-25 Lakhs

**Pros:**
- Single product for insurance + investment
- Tax-free maturity (Section 10(10D))
- Can switch between funds free of cost
- Loyalty additions from year 6 onwards
- Good for disciplined long-term investors (15+ years)

**Cons:**
- Complex, hard to track performance
- Higher charges than pure mutual funds
- Life cover may be inadequate for your needs
- Surrender penalty if exited before 5 years

**Best ULIP Plans 2024:**
- HDFC Life Click 2 Wealth
- ICICI Pru Life iProtect Smart + invest in MF
- Max Life Online Savings Plan
- Bajaj Allianz Goal Assure II

### ENDOWMENT PLAN

**What it is:**
Traditional savings-cum-protection plan. You pay premium for 20-30 years. On maturity OR death, you/your nominee gets Sum Assured + bonus.

**Pros:**
- Guaranteed returns (no market risk)
- Disciplined savings
- Tax benefits on premium and maturity

**Cons:**
- Returns are very low (4-5% effective yield)
- Long lock-in period (15-30 years)
- Surrender value is very low in early years
- Better returns available in PPF, FD, Mutual Funds

**Verdict:** Endowment plans are generally NOT recommended today. Their returns don't beat inflation. Better to separate insurance (Term) and investment (MF/PPF).

## The "Buy Term + Invest the Rest" Strategy

**Example: 30-year-old, budget Rs. 50,000/year**

### Option A: Endowment Plan
- Premium: Rs. 50,000/year for 25 years
- Maturity at 55: ~Rs. 22 Lakhs
- Effective return: ~5% p.a.

### Option B: Term + Mutual Fund
- Term Insurance: Rs. 12,000/year (Rs. 1Cr cover)
- SIP in Equity MF: Rs. 38,000/year for 25 years
- Maturity at 55: ~Rs. 1.5-2 Crores (at 12% CAGR)
- PLUS Rs. 1 Crore life cover throughout!

**Winner: Option B gives 3-4x more wealth + better protection**

## Who Should Buy What?

| Profile | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| Age 25-35, just married | Term Insurance (mandatory) + SIP in MF |
| Age 35-45, high income, 30% tax bracket | Term + ULIP (for tax-free corpus) |
| Age 45+, risk-averse | Endowment or annuity plan |
| Business owner | High cover Term (replace income for family) |
| NRI | Term insurance with Return of Premium option |

## FAQ

**Q: I already have group insurance from my employer. Do I need term insurance?**
A: YES. Group insurance ends if you lose the job. Always have personal term insurance.

**Q: What is Return of Premium (ROP) Term Plan?**
A: You get all premiums back if you survive the term. But premium is 2-3x higher. Usually NOT worth it. Better to buy normal term and invest the difference.

**Q: Can I have multiple term insurance policies?**
A: Yes. Many people have multiple policies from different insurers for diversification.

**Q: What is Accidental Death Benefit Rider?**
A: Extra cover paid if death is due to accident. Usually doubles the payout. Very affordable rider.
