MOTOR INSURANCE - COMPLETE GUIDE
=================================

WHAT IS MOTOR INSURANCE?
Motor insurance is a policy that provides financial protection against losses arising from accidents, theft, natural calamities, or third-party liabilities involving your vehicle. Under the Motor Vehicles Act 1988, Third-Party (TP) insurance is MANDATORY for all vehicles in India.

TYPES OF MOTOR INSURANCE
--------------------------

1. THIRD-PARTY LIABILITY (Mandatory by Law)
   - Covers bodily injury or death to a third party
   - Covers property damage to a third party (up to Rs. 7.5 Lakh)
   - Does NOT cover your own vehicle damage
   - Premium is fixed by IRDAI (not negotiable)
   - TP Premium for private car (1000-1500cc): Rs. 3,416/year

2. OWN DAMAGE (OD) COVER
   - Covers damage to YOUR vehicle
   - Fire, theft, flood, cyclone, earthquake
   - Accidental collision damage
   - Premium depends on IDV, make, model, age of vehicle
   - Optional but strongly recommended

3. COMPREHENSIVE POLICY (TP + OD = Package Policy)
   - Combines both Third-Party and Own Damage
   - Most complete protection
   - Recommended for all vehicles less than 10 years old

4. STANDALONE OD POLICY
   - Only Own Damage, no TP
   - Useful if you have TP from another insurer
   - Introduced in 2019 by IRDAI

UNDERSTANDING IDV (INSURED DECLARED VALUE)
-------------------------------------------
IDV = Current Market Value of Vehicle (after depreciation)
This is the MAXIMUM you will receive in a total loss / theft claim.

Age of Vehicle     Depreciation    IDV (for Rs. 10L car)
Up to 6 months     5%              Rs. 9,50,000
6 months-1 year    15%             Rs. 8,50,000
1-2 years          20%             Rs. 8,00,000
2-3 years          30%             Rs. 7,00,000
3-4 years          40%             Rs. 6,00,000
4-5 years          50%             Rs. 5,00,000
Above 5 years      Mutually agreed Rs. 3,00,000 (approx)

TIP: Always choose a higher IDV - it costs only slightly more premium but gives better coverage.

NO CLAIM BONUS (NCB)
---------------------
If you do not make any claim in a policy year, you earn NCB discount on your OD premium at renewal.

Claim-Free Years    NCB Discount
1 year              20%
2 years             25%
3 years             35%
4 years             45%
5+ years            50%

IMPORTANT: NCB belongs to YOU, not the vehicle. You can transfer it to a new vehicle.
NCB can save you up to 50% on OD premium after 5 claim-free years!

KEY ADD-ON COVERS
------------------
1. Zero Depreciation (Bumper to Bumper):
   - Without it: Insurer pays claim minus depreciation on parts
   - With it: Insurer pays FULL cost of parts without depreciation deduction
   - Highly recommended for cars less than 5 years old
   - Extra premium: approx 15-20% of OD premium

2. Engine Protection:
   - Covers engine damage due to water ingression (flooding)
   - Covers hydrostatic lock, leakage of lubricating oil
   - Essential if you live in flood-prone areas
   
3. Return to Invoice (RTI):
   - In total loss/theft: pays original invoice price, not IDV
   - Covers registration and road tax too
   - Recommended for first 3 years

4. Roadside Assistance (RSA):
   - 24x7 help for breakdown, flat tyre, fuel delivery
   - Towing to nearest garage
   - Very affordable add-on (Rs. 200-500/year)

5. NCB Protect:
   - Allows 1 claim per year without losing NCB
   - Worth buying if you have 35%+ NCB

WHAT IS NOT COVERED (EXCLUSIONS)
----------------------------------
- Drunk driving or influence of drugs
- Driving without valid driving license
- Using private vehicle for commercial purpose
- Electrical/mechanical breakdown
- Depreciation and wear & tear
- Outside India travel (without international cover)
- Deliberate damage
- War, nuclear risks

TWO-WHEELER INSURANCE
-----------------------
All rules are same but premiums are lower.
Third-Party Premium (up to 75cc): Rs. 538/year (IRDAI fixed rate)
Third-Party Premium (75cc-150cc): Rs. 714/year
Third-Party Premium (150cc-350cc): Rs. 1,366/year
Third-Party Premium (above 350cc): Rs. 2,804/year

HOW TO RENEW ON TIME
---------------------
- Renew before expiry date
- If policy lapses, vehicle must be inspected before renewal
- You LOSE your NCB if not renewed within 90 days of expiry
- Third-party lapse = driving illegally = heavy fine

TIPS TO REDUCE PREMIUM
------------------------
1. Install anti-theft device (ARAI approved) - 2.5% discount
2. Be part of automobile association - 5% discount
3. Opt for voluntary deductible - saves 5-25% on OD premium
4. Choose only needed add-ons
5. Compare quotes on Policybazaar, Coverfox, InsuranceDekho
